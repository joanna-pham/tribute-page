Ruth Bader Ginsburg

March 15, 1933: born in Brooklyn, New, York

1955: applied to Havard School of Law with husband

1959: became law clerk to a judge in Southern District of New York

1972: became first tenured female professor at Columbia Law School

1993: sworn in as Supreme Court justice filling the seat held by Justice Byron White

2002: inducted into the National Women's Hall of Fame

2010: receives the American Bar Association's highest honor, the ABA medal

2013: first Supreme Court justice to officiate at a same-sex marriage ceremony

September 18, 2020: dies due to complications of metastatic pancreatic cancer
